What did I learn?

We learned about words with “O” and “OO” and their pronunciation.

Single “O”
👉 When the letter “O” is followed just by consonants, “O” does not say its name. It makes a short “aw” sound.

👉 When the letter “O” is followed by a consonant and a silent “E,” the letter “O” says its name. It makes a long “O” sound.

👉 When the letter “O” is followed by another vowel, the two vowels make a vowel team. In vowel teams that start with “O,” the letter “O” says its name. It makes a long “O” sound. The other vowel is silent.
